How to use this auditor

Why Most Creators Never Know Where They Stand

Every week, thousands of creators post consistently, grow their followings, and still wonder why the money isn't showing up. The uncomfortable truth? Follower counts and views mean very different things depending on your niche — and most creators are measuring themselves against the wrong benchmarks entirely.

A Finance creator with 25 000 followers can genuinely earn more per month than a Comedy creator with 500 000. Why? Because brands pay for attention quality, not just attention quantity. The niche you operate in directly dictates your CPM rates, sponsorship values, and the follower threshold needed to attract consistent brand deals.

What this tool actually calculates: Your current monthly earning potential (ad revenue + sponsorship estimate), your influencer readiness score as a percentage, the exact follower count and view count targets for your specific niche, and how far you currently are from that sustainable income threshold.

How to use it correctly: Enter your total follower count from your primary platform. For average views, take your last 10 posts, add up the view counts, and divide by 10 — this gives the most accurate average. Then select the niche that best describes your content. If you mix tech and business content, pick the one that represents the majority of your posts.

Hit Run Full Audit. The system will cross-reference your stats against industry rate cards and niche-specific engagement benchmarks. Within seconds, you'll get a full breakdown — including a circle readiness chart showing exactly how close you are to the income milestone for your niche, colour-coded so you can instantly see your safe zone and risk zone.

If your score is low, don't panic. A low score is not a verdict — it's a precise target. Most creators who fail do so because they had no idea where the finish line was. Now you do. Come back every 30 days, re-enter your updated numbers, and use the results as your personal KPI dashboard. This is how you build systematically instead of just hoping to go viral.
